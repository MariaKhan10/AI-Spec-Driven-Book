## Core Principles

### I. Decision Point Mapping

*   Content must force students to reason about physical intelligence (e.g., ‚ÄúWhat sensory data is necessary for a humanoid to climb stairs?‚Äù).
*   Teaching methods must evolve through layers: Embodied Intuition, Mechanical & Control Reasoning, AI-driven decision loops, Full-system humanoid design.

*   Avoid overly prescriptive instructions (e.g., fixed torque thresholds) or vague inspiration (e.g., ‚Äúmake the robot move smoothly.‚Äù).

### V. Decision Frameworks Over Rules
*   Prioritize teaching decision frameworks over rigid rules.

### VII. Feedback Loops and Iteration
*   Design learning activities that incorporate explicit feedback mechanisms and iterative refinement, mirroring the development cycle of embodied systems.
*   Encourage students to continuously evaluate, debug, and improve their understanding and implementations.

### VIII. Terminology Consistency
*   Maintain a consistent and precise terminology throughout the textbook to avoid ambiguity and foster clear communication in Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.
*   Provide a glossary or clear definitions for key terms to ensure a unified understanding across all chapters.

### IX. Practical Application and Project-Based Learning
*   Prioritize hands-on exercises, simulations, and real-world projects that allow students to apply theoretical knowledge directly to Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics challenges.
